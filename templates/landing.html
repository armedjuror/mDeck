This started with a specific problem.

My mother teaches higher-secondary mathematics using a textbook and a board. She kept asking me to make teaching decks for her chapters. PowerPoint took about 40 minutes per chapter. Other AI tools produced slides as images — fine at first glance, but impossible to fix when a formula was wrong, and the AI had to regenerate the whole slide to change a single symbol.

The solution was straightforward: give Claude a chapter PDF and mDeck's AI connection, and get back a markdown deck. Every formula is a text string. Every fix is a text edit. Human and AI keep working on the same file.

Every formula is a text string you can edit. No regenerating images. No copy-pasting from a screenshot. The AI and you work on the same file.
